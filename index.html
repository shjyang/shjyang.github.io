About Me

I am an assistant professor in the Department of Applied Mathematics of Fluminense Federal University (UFF). Before that, I was a postdoctoral fellow hosted by Prof. Leonardo T. Rolla in São Paulo University, hosted by Prof. Gidi Amir and Dr. Naomi Feldheim in Bar-Ilan University, hosted by Prof. Tertuliano Franco in Federal University of Bahia, and a Ph.D. student under the supervision of Prof. Hubert Lacoin at Instituto de Matemática Pura e Aplicada. Here are my CV and thesis: Mixing time for interface models and particle system (slide).

I am mainly interested in the intersection between probability theory and statistical mechanics. In particular, my research focuses on Markov chains and mixing times, random polymer models, interacting particle systems, random walks in random environments, Gaussian random functions, etc.
